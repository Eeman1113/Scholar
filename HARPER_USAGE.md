# Harper.js Grammar & Spelling Check - User Guide

Scholar now includes advanced grammar and spelling checking powered by Harper.js, a privacy-first grammar checker that works entirely offline.

## Features

### ✅ **Privacy First**
- All grammar checking happens locally on your device
- No text is sent to external servers
- Works completely offline

### ⚡ **Real-time Checking**
- Grammar and spelling errors are highlighted as you type
- Fast suggestions appear in under 10 milliseconds
- Non-blocking processing won't slow down your writing

### 🎯 **Visual Error Indicators**
- **Red wavy underline**: Spelling errors
- **Yellow wavy underline**: Grammar issues  
- **Blue wavy underline**: Style suggestions

### 💡 **Smart Suggestions**
- Click on any underlined error to see suggestions
- Apply fixes with a single click
- Add words to your personal dictionary
- Ignore suggestions you don't want to see again

## How to Use

### 1. **Enable Grammar Checking**
- Look for the "Grammar" button in the editor toolbar
- Click it to toggle grammar checking on/off
- When enabled, you'll see a checkmark or error count

### 2. **Fix Grammar Errors**
- Underlined text indicates grammar or spelling issues
- Click on any underlined word or phrase
- A popup will appear with:
  - Description of the issue
  - Suggested corrections
  - Options to ignore or add to dictionary

### 3. **Apply Suggestions**
- Click any suggestion to automatically apply it
- The text will be corrected instantly
- The error indicator will disappear

### 4. **Manage Your Dictionary**
- For spelling errors, you can "Add to Dictionary"
- This prevents the same word from being flagged again
- Useful for proper names, technical terms, etc.

### 5. **Ignore Suggestions**
- If you disagree with a suggestion, click "Ignore this suggestion"
- Harper will stop suggesting that specific correction
- Your personal preferences are remembered

## Status Indicators

The grammar status in the toolbar shows:

- **"Grammar: Good"** ✅ - No issues found
- **"Grammar: X issues"** ⚠️ - Number of problems detected
- **"Grammar: Off"** 📖 - Grammar checking disabled
- **"Checking..."** ⏳ - Currently analyzing text

Red/yellow/blue badges show counts by severity level.

## Tips for Best Results

### ✍️ **Write Naturally**
- Harper works best with complete sentences
- It understands context and writing style
- Don't worry about errors while drafting - fix them later

### 📝 **Review Suggestions**
- Not all suggestions need to be applied
- Harper helps improve clarity, not enforce rules
- Use your judgment for creative or intentional style choices

### ⚙️ **Customize Your Experience**
- Add technical terms to your dictionary
- Ignore suggestions for your writing style
- Toggle off during brainstorming, on during editing

### Spelling Errors

## Keyboard Shortcuts

- **Esc**: Close suggestion popup
- **Tab**: Accept AI writing suggestions (different feature)
- **Click**: Open grammar suggestions for underlined text

## Privacy & Performance

- **100% Private**: No data leaves your device
- **Offline Ready**: Works without internet
- **Fast Processing**: Uses WebAssembly for speed
- **Non-blocking**: Won't interrupt your writing flow

## Troubleshooting

### Grammar checking not working?
1. Check that it's enabled in the toolbar
2. Try typing a sentence with an obvious error
3. Wait a moment for processing (1-2 seconds after typing)

### Suggestions not appearing?
1. Make sure you're clicking directly on underlined text
2. Check that the popup isn't hidden behind other elements
3. Try refreshing the page if issues persist

### Performance issues?
1. Grammar checking uses Web Workers for smooth performance
2. If you experience slowness, try disabling temporarily
3. Large documents may take a moment to process fully
